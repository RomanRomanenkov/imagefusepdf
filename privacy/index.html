Privacy Policy

Last updated: November 28, 2025

This Privacy Policy describes how ImageFuse PDF (the "Extension", "we", "us", or "our") handles information when you install and use it in your browser.

By installing and using ImageFuse PDF, you acknowledge that you have read and understood this Privacy Policy and agree to its terms. If you do not agree, please uninstall the Extension and discontinue its use.

1. Who We Are & How to Contact Us

ImageFuse PDF is a browser extension that allows you to convert images to PDF directly in your browser.

  • ImageFuse PDF
  • Contact email: imagefusepdf@gmail.com
  • Website: https://imagefusepdf.com

If you have any questions about this Privacy Policy, you can contact us using the email above.

2. Scope of this Policy

This Privacy Policy applies to the ImageFuse PDF browser extension only.

It does not apply to:

  • Websites you visit while using your browser
  • Third-party services where you may upload or share PDFs created with the Extension (e.g. email providers, cloud storage, messaging apps)
  • The browser vendor (such as Google Chrome) or the extension store itself and any data they collect under their own policies

Please review the privacy policies of any such third parties separately.

3. Summary – Key Principles

  • The Extension is designed with a local-first / no-server approach.
  • Your images and generated PDFs are processed locally in your browser and are not sent to our servers.
  • We do not collect, sell, or rent your personal information via the Extension.
  • We do not use third-party analytics, tracking pixels, or advertising networks within the Extension.

The rest of this Privacy Policy explains these points in more detail.

4. Information We Process While You Use the Extension

4.1 Images and PDFs

When you use ImageFuse PDF, you may add images in various ways (for example, by selecting files from your device or using a context menu on an image in a web page).

To provide the core functionality, the Extension may temporarily handle:

  • The image files you choose to convert (e.g., JPG, PNG, GIF, WebP, etc.)
  • Basic image-related information such as file name, size, and dimensions, only as needed to generate the PDF
  • The PDF file generated from those images

All of this processing happens within your browser on your device. The Extension does not upload these images or PDFs to our servers.

4.2 Local Storage in the Browser

To make the Extension convenient to use, it may store certain information in your browser's local extension storage, such as:

  • The list of images you have added and their order
  • Thumbnail previews of images (where applicable)
  • Your PDF configuration or preferences (e.g., page size, orientation, margins)

This information is stored only in your browser profile on your device and is used solely to provide and improve the functionality of the Extension (for example, restoring your working session or remembering preferences).

We do not receive this data, and it is not shared with third parties by us.

You can clear this data at any time by:

  • Removing images or using "clear" functions within the Extension; and/or
  • Clearing extension data/cache via your browser's settings; and/or
  • Uninstalling the Extension.

5. Information We Do Not Collect

Through the Extension itself, we:

  • Do not collect your name, email address, phone number, or contact details.
  • Do not collect your browsing history or track the websites you visit.
  • Do not inspect or monitor the content of web pages you view, beyond what is necessary when you explicitly use the Extension on a specific image.
  • Do not upload your images, generated PDFs, or related metadata to our servers.
  • Do not include third-party analytics, advertising SDKs, or tracking technologies inside the Extension.

The only personal data we may have about you is information you voluntarily provide if you contact us (for example, by email for support). In that case, we will use this information only to respond to your inquiry and maintain basic records where legally required.

6. Permissions and How They Are Used

When you install ImageFuse PDF, your browser may ask you to grant certain permissions. These permissions are requested exclusively to provide the features you expect from an image-to-PDF extension.

Typical permissions and their purpose may include:

  • Access to data on websites you visit (limited to images you interact with using the Extension): Needed so that, when you explicitly choose the Extension's action on an image (for example, via a right-click "Save to PDF" menu), it can access that particular image to add it to your PDF.
  • Storage permission (extension storage): Used to store images you've added, their order, and your configuration settings locally in your browser, so your workflow persists between sessions.
  • UI / side panel or similar interface permissions: Required so the Extension can display its interface, show previews, and let you manage your images and PDF options.

We only request the minimum set of permissions that is reasonably necessary for the Extension to function as described.

7. Third Parties, Analytics, and Advertising

  • The Extension does not use third-party analytics or tracking services.
  • The Extension does not display third-party advertisements.
  • The Extension does not share your images, PDFs, or related metadata with third parties.

If you choose to share or upload your generated PDFs using other services (e.g., by email or cloud storage), that is entirely under your control and is governed by the privacy policies of those services.

8. Browser Vendors and Extension Stores

Your browser vendor (for example, Google for Chrome) and the extension store may collect their own diagnostic or usage information such as:

  • Install and uninstall counts
  • Crash reports, performance metrics, or error logs
  • Aggregate usage statistics

These are handled under the privacy policies of the respective vendors and stores and are separate from the functioning of the Extension itself.

We may have access to aggregated, anonymized statistics provided by the store (such as total number of installs or basic error reports). These reports do not identify individual users.

9. Legal Bases for Processing (EEA/UK Users)

If you are located in the European Economic Area (EEA) or the United Kingdom, our processing of personal data (which, in practice, is very minimal) is based on the following legal grounds:

  • Performance of a contract: To provide you with the Extension and its core functionality (image-to-PDF conversion).
  • Legitimate interest: To maintain and improve the Extension, ensure security, and respond to support requests.

Because the Extension does not send usage data or images to our servers, these legal bases primarily apply if and when you contact us directly and provide personal data in that context.

Your Rights

If you are a resident of California, you may have additional rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) and the California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA), including:

  • The right to non-discrimination for exercising these rights

ImageFuse PDF does not sell or share personal information as those terms are defined under the CCPA/CPRA, and the Extension does not collect usage data or images on our servers.

If you contact us directly and provide personal information, we will handle any requests relating to that information in line with applicable law. You can contact us at imagefusepdf@gmail.com.

11. Children's Privacy

The Extension is intended for general-purpose use and is not directed to children or designed for use by individuals under the age at which they can provide consent to data processing in their jurisdiction (for example, 13 or 16 years old, depending on the country).

We do not knowingly collect personal information from children through the Extension. If you believe that a child has provided personal information to us, please contact us so that we can take appropriate steps to delete such information if necessary.

12. Data Security

Because ImageFuse PDF processes images and generates PDFs directly in your browser:

  • Your images and PDFs are kept on your device and under your control.
  • Data stored by the Extension is contained in your browser's extension storage area.

We aim to follow good security practices in developing and maintaining the Extension. However, no software system is completely secure. You are responsible for:

  • Keeping your devices secure
  • Using supported browser versions
  • Applying security patches and updates offered by your browser and operating system

If we become aware of a security vulnerability in the Extension that may affect user privacy, we will take reasonable steps to address it (for example, by issuing an update via the extension store).

13. Data Retention and Deletion

As a general rule:

  • We do not store your images or PDFs on our servers.
  • Data used by the Extension is kept locally in your browser storage only for as long as it is needed to provide the functionality (e.g., while images are added and until you clear them or uninstall the Extension).

You can delete locally stored data by:

  • Removing images and using "clear" or similar functions in the Extension;
  • Clearing your browser's extension data or cache via browser settings;
  • Uninstalling the Extension.

If you contact us and provide personal data (for example, by email), we may retain that communication for as long as reasonably necessary for support, legal, or record-keeping purposes, in accordance with applicable law.
